Emily Herron is a Data Science and Engineering Ph.D. candidate in [The Bredesen Center](https://bredesencenter.utk.edu/) at the University of Tennessee, Knoxville, under the guidance of Dr. Steven R. Young. 
She is also a graduate research assistant in the [Learning Systems Group](https://www.ornl.gov/group/learning-systems) at [Oak Ridge National Laboratory](https://www.ornl.gov). 
Her dissertation research involves developing scalable and stable generalized differentiable neural architecture search methods.
Her previous research has also involved evolutionary algorithms, neural network ensembles,  text mining, and NLP models. She is a member of ACM and IEEE and has served as a reviewer for the International Conference on Machine Learning (ICML) and as a peer mentor in the Bredesen Center. 
Emily also earned a B.S. in Computational Science from Mercer University in 2014.
